About

Robert W. McGee

Robert W. McGee was born in Erie, Pennsylvania, just south of the Canadian border. He has lived in Ohio, Chicago, New Jersey, Armenia, Bosnia, Florida, and North Carolina. He has a total of twenty three degrees, 13 of which are doctorates from universities in the United States and four European countries. His degrees include accounting, finance, politics, law, philosophy, economics, international economics, applied global ethics, and social science to name a few.

He has worked as a high school math teacher, accountant, tax attorney, editor, professor, consultant, and entrepreneur. Some of his former clients include the United States Agency for International Development, World Bank, African Development Bank, and the Central Intelligence Agency.

He has published 58 non-fiction books and more than 700 articles in countless disciplines.

A martial arts enthusiast, he has won medals in both the Shukokai (Japanese) and Taekwondo (Korean) styles, including 18 gold and 9 silver medals in National Taekwondo Championship tournaments.

He recently embarked on a new journey as a fiction writer. Justifiable Homicide: A Political Thriller marked his debut in the genre. To date he has also written 1 novelette: Annie and the Senator: A Story of Vigilante Justice and 1 short story: The TSA versus the People: A Short Story of Vengeance. He has several novels and shorter works in process.
